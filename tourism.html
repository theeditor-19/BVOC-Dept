BVoc Tourism and Hospitality Management

The basic objective of the Bachelor of Vocation degree programme in Tourism and Hospitality is to provide to the hospitality industry
steady stream of competent young men and women with the necessary knowledge, skills, values and attitudes to occupy key operational positions.


Eligibility for Admission

Eligibility for admissions and reservation of seats for BVoc Tourism and Hospitality Management shall be according to the rules framed by the University from time to time. No student shall be eligible for admission to BVoc T&HM unless he/she has passed the Plus Two of the Higher Secondary Board of Kerala or that of any other university or Board of Examinations in any state recognized as equivalent to the Plus Two of the Higher Secondary Board in Kerala, with not less than 45 % marks in aggregate. However SC/ST, OBC, and other eligible communities shall be given relaxation as per University rules. Those who passed Vocational Higher Secondary course will get a weightage of 25 marks

Programme Structure

The B.Voc Tourism shall include:

1. Language courses (English)
2. Foreign Langauge course (German)
3. General Education Components
4. Skill Components
5. Project
6. Internship
7. Industrial Training
8. Familiarisation Trips
9. Soft Skills and Personality Development Programmes
10. Study tours

Nature of the Course

This course follows 2 (b) pattern of the University under first degree CBCS program with appropriate modifications.

1. Understand and demonstrate the core technical, analytical, and conceptual skills appropriate for hospitality.
2. Comprehend and articulate written and oral communication as appropriate for hospitality environments.
3. Understand the concepts and application of managerial, financial, computer and technical skills that are needed to be successful within the hospitality industry
4. Demonstrate ability to apply strategies for managing diverse, multicultural hospitality.
5. Prepare hospitality undergraduate candidates for entry level management positions with a specific focus on individual, social and environmental perspectives.
6. Apply the knowledge gained to manage and evaluate functional systems in hospitality and lodging operations

Duration

The duration of the B. Voc Tourism shall be three years consisting of six semesters. There shall be at least 90 working days in a semester and a minimum 450 hours of instruction. As per the UGC guidelines, there are multiple exit point for a candidate admitted in this course. If he/she is completing all the six credits successfully, he/she will get BVoc degree in Tourism & Hospitality Management. BVoc degree holder is expected to acquire the skills needed for a tour operator or entrepreneur.
